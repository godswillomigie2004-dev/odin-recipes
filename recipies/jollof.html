Description

Jollof rice is a legendary West African one-pot dish known for its vibrant red color and deep, savory flavor. The secret lies in a rich "stew" base made from blended bell peppers, tomatoes, onions, and scotch bonnets, which are fried together with spices like curry, thyme, and bay leaves until fragrant and caramelized.

The magic happens when the rice is steamed slowly inside this concentrated tomato base. By sealing the pot tightly, the grains absorb the spicy stock and smoky aroma, resulting in a perfectly tender texture. For many, the best part is the slightly charred rice at the bottom of the pot, which provides that authentic "party-style" smokiness.

Steps

  1. Blend the tomatoes, peppers, and onion, then boil until thick.
  2. Fry sliced onions, tomato paste, and spices in oil.
  3. Add the pepper base and fry until the oil separates.
  4. Stir in the rice and stock, then cover tightly with foil.
  5. Steam on low heat for 20-30 minutes.
  6. Add butter, fluff with a fork, and serve.
